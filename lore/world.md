# The World of Glacier Moon

## Kepler-442b

**Designation:** Kepler-442b  
**Classification:** Super-Earth (1.3x Earth mass)  
**Orbital Period:** 112 days  
**Surface Temperature:** -42°C average  
**Atmosphere:** Thin CO₂/N₂ mix, breathable with rebreather assistance  
**Liquid Water:** Present as subglacial oceans beneath 2-4km ice sheets  

### Geography

The surface of Kepler-442b is dominated by **glacier fields**—vast plains of crystallized water ice mixed with frozen methane and ammonia deposits. These fields are not static; they flow at geological speeds, creating pressure ridges and crevasses that shift over decades.

**Key Regions:**
- **Grid 7-Alpha:** Primary outpost sector. Flat glacier plain suitable for landing.
- **Sector 7-Gamma:** Eastern ridge region. Unstable ice, high mineral content.
- **The Deep:** Subglacial ocean network. Unknown extent. Potentially planet-wide.

### The Spires

Ancient crystalline structures that predate human arrival by millions of years. Initially dismissed as geological formations, the Spires are now understood to be **biological architecture**—grown, not built, by the planet's original inhabitants.

### The Awakening

The arrival of human technology (drilling, reactor heat, electromagnetic emissions) appears to have triggered a dormant response in the planet's native systems. The Spires are "waking up"—growing, pulsing, and communicating.
