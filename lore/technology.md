# Technology & Equipment

## Human Technology

### Transport Vessels
- **Atmospheric Transport:** Single-stage shuttles capable of surface-to-orbit transit. Magnetic landing systems.
- **Surface Vehicles:** Tracked crawlers for glacier traversal. Limited range (200km on full charge).

### Life Support
- **Thermal Suits:** Standard issue for GL-7 personnel. Rated to -60°C for 12 hours.
- **Rebreathers:** CO₂ scrubbing systems for thin-atmosphere operations.
- **Cryo-Pods:** Long-term hibernation for interstellar transit. 847-day standard cycles.

### Communications
- **Comm Array:** Orbital relay station. Primary link to Earth. Currently disabled at GL-7.
- **Short-Range Radio:** 50km effective range. Subject to magnetic interference.
- **Emergency Beacons:** Automated distress signals. Battery life: 72 hours.

### Mining Equipment
- **Deep Drill:** Capable of 4km penetration. Uses focused thermal lances to melt ice.
- **Mineral Extractor:** Separates valuable compounds from ice matrix.

## Resonant Technology

The Resonant do not use "technology" in the human sense. Their tools are:

- **Crystal Architecture:** Grown structures that serve as computation, communication, and memory storage
- **Temporal Manipulation:** Ability to project sensory information across time
- **Neural Interface:** Direct stimulation of biological nervous systems without physical contact
- **Planetary Engineering:** The ability to reshape ice and crystal formations at continental scales

## Key Artifacts

- **The Dais:** Central platform in the subglacial chamber. Appears to be an interface terminal.
- **Fenwick's Tablet:** Last human technology to record Resonant activity. Data partially corrupted.
