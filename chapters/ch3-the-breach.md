> **NARRATION**
> The breach was not ice. It was bone. Or something that looked like bone—porous, pale, threaded with veins of silver light. The passage breathed. Warm air pushed out, then sucked back in, rhythmic, ancient.

> **NARRATION**
> Elara's scanner flatlined, then rebooted, then flatlined again. "The readings are impossible. This structure is organic. It's alive, Kael. The entire moon might be alive."

> **NARRATION**
> They descended for what felt like hours. The walls pulsed with bioluminescence—soft blue, then angry red, then a color that had no name. Kael felt it in his teeth, behind his eyes.

> **NARRATION**
> Then the passage opened into a chamber, and they saw what the moon had been growing for ten million years.

> **DIALOGUE: Sera Voss** *terrified*
> "My med-scanner... it's reading this as a womb. Commander, we're inside something's womb."

> **DIALOGUE: Kael Vance** *steadfast*
> "Steady. Weapons hot. Whatever's in here, we face it together."

> **NARRATION**
> The walls convulsed. A shape detached from the ceiling—wet, glistening, unfolding like a flower made of teeth and light. It had no eyes, but it looked at them. It had no mouth, but it spoke.

> **DIALOGUE: The Entity** *hungry*
> "Warm. Small. You smell of star-dust and fear. We have waited so long for new hosts."
