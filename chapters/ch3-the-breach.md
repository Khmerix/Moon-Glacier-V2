> **NARRATION**
> The breach was not ice. It was meat. Or something that had been meat, once—porous, pale, threaded with veins of silver light that pulsed in rhythms that matched no heartbeat Kael had ever heard.

> **NARRATION**
> Elara's scanner flatlined, then rebooted, then flatlined again. "The readings are impossible. This structure is organic. It's alive, Kael. The entire moon might be alive."

> **NARRATION**
> They descended for what felt like hours. The walls pulsed with bioluminescence—soft blue, then angry red, then a color that made Kael's eyes water and his stomach clench. The air was warm. Humid. It smelled like a womb. Like a mouth.

> **NARRATION**
> Then the passage opened into a chamber, and they saw what the moon had been growing for ten million years.

> **NARRATION**
> The chamber was a stomach. Or a heart. Or both. Vast, wet, lined with villi the size of skyscrapers that swayed in currents of warm air. The floor was carpeted in translucent eggs, each one large enough to hold a human body, each one containing a shadow that twitched and turned.

> **DIALOGUE: Sera Voss** *terrified*
> "My med-scanner... it's reading this as a womb. Commander, we're inside something's womb. And those eggs... those eggs are full of people."

> **NARRATION**
> She was right. Through the translucent membrane of the nearest egg, Kael could see a face. A man in a UN uniform, eyes closed, breathing slow. Crystal filaments growing from his pores, weaving him into the egg wall. Feeding him. Changing him.

> **DIALOGUE: Kael Vance** *steadfast*
> "Steady. Weapons hot. Whatever's in here, we face it together."

> **NARRATION**
> The walls convulsed. A shape detached from the ceiling—wet, glistening, unfolding like a flower made of teeth and light and things that had no name. It had no eyes, but it looked at them. It had no mouth, but it spoke.

> **DIALOGUE: The Entity** *hungry*
> "Warm. Small. You smell of star-dust and fear. We have waited so long for new hosts."
