> **NARRATION**
> They made it back to the outpost with the crystal samples. Elara set up a containment field—standard protocol for unknown xenomaterials. Standard. Safe. Predictable.

> **NARRATION**
> The crystals had other plans.

> **NARRATION**
> At 0200 hours, every screen in the lab flickered simultaneously. Not with static. With patterns. Mathematical, musical, biological. The crystals were transmitting. And they were using human technology as their voice.

> **DIALOGUE: Elara Synn** *awestruck*
> "It's not random. It's a language. A language older than carbon. And it's teaching me. I can feel it, Kael. Like someone pouring starlight directly into my mind."

> **NARRATION**
> Her eyes were wrong. Too bright. The pupils had shifted from black to deep violet, flecked with silver.

> **DIALOGUE: Marcus Thorne** *alarmed*
> "Commander, her neural activity is off the charts. Whatever's in those crystals, it's rewriting her. We need to destroy them. Now."

> **DIALOGUE: Elara Synn** *distant*
> "No. It's not rewriting. It's remembering. I remember things now. The war. The last war. The one that turned this moon into a graveyard."

> **NARRATION**
> The lights died. In the darkness, the crystals sang. And something answered from very far away.
