> **NARRATION**
> Kael signed the contract. His team lived. The entity remained undisturbed—for now. And Helix Corp gained exclusive rights to "xenological consultation services."

> **NARRATION**
> The wording was careful. The lawyers had been thorough. But Kael knew the truth: he had sold humanity's first contact to a corporation that measured lives in quarterly earnings.

> **DIALOGUE: Helix Director** *satisfied*
> "You've made the right choice, Commander. Your team goes home. You get a generous stipend. And the entity... well. We'll take good care of it."

> **NARRATION**
> Kael looked at the entity through the observation glass. It looked back. And in its ancient eyes, he saw something he hadn't expected.

> **NARRATION**
> Pity.

> **DIALOGUE: The Entity** *whispered*
> "They will try to own me. They will fail. But you, Commander... you will have to live with the choice."

> **NARRATION**
> Kael lived with it for forty years. It never got easier.
