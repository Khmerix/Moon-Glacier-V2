> **NARRATION**
> The residential sector smelled wrong. Not just stale air and frozen lubricant. It smelled like a butcher's freezer left too long without power—sweet and coppery, clinging to the back of the throat.

> **NARRATION**
> They found the first body in the mess hall. A woman in a UN captain's uniform, frozen mid-scream, her mouth open so wide the jaw had dislocated. Her name tag read: TANAKA.

> **DIALOGUE: Elara Synn** *shaking*
> "She's been dead for months. But the ice... the ice is growing through her. Look at her hands, Kael. Look at what it's doing to her hands."

> **NARRATION**
> Crystal filaments extruded from Tanaka's fingertips, weaving through the air like frozen lightning, merging her corpse with the wall. She wasn't buried in the ice. She had become part of it.

> **NARRATION**
> Sera checked the other bodies. Six of them. Same condition. Same crystal fusion. One had tried to shoot himself—the pistol was still frozen in his mouth, the back of his skull a hollowed cave of blue glass.

> **DIALOGUE: Sera Voss** *numb*
> "They didn't starve. They didn't freeze. They were... harvested. Something took them piece by piece and replaced them with this."

> **NARRATION**
> Nyx found the log. Tanaka's final entry, recorded three hours before the crystals claimed her vocal cords.

> **DIALOGUE: Captain Yuki Tanaka** *recording*
> "If someone finds this—don't trust the singing. It sounds beautiful. It sounds like your mother calling you home. But it's not singing. It's digestion. It's the sound of something eating you from the inside out. Dr. Fenwick was right. God help us, Fenwick was right."

> **NARRATION**
> The log ended in static. Then screaming. Then silence.

> **DIALOGUE: Kael Vance** *hardened*
> "We bury nothing. We document everything. And we find whatever did this."
