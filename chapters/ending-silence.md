> **NARRATION**
> They stayed. Not as conquerors. Not as students. As guardians.

> **NARRATION**
> Kael rotated the team in shifts—two weeks on the ice, two weeks in orbit, endless cycles of vigilance. They built watchtowers from outpost scrap. They laid sensors across every approach. They became the wall between the entity and a humanity that wasn't ready.

> **DIALOGUE: Nyx Oriel** *resigned*
> "We're not soldiers anymore. We're locks on a door nobody asked us to guard."

> **DIALOGUE: Kael Vance** *determined*
> "Someone has to be. Until the world grows up enough to knock politely."

> **NARRATION**
> Years passed. The entity remained dormant, patient, dreaming. Occasionally, a Helix scout ship would approach. Kael would send a warning. Sometimes they listened. Sometimes they didn't.

> **NARRATION**
> The glacier grew over the outpost. The team aged. New recruits arrived, heard the stories, and chose to stay.

> **NARRATION**
> Commander Kael Vance died an old man, buried in the ice he had sworn to protect. And on the day of his death, the entity sang for him. A single, beautiful note that echoed across the empty stars.

> **NARRATION**
> Thank you.
