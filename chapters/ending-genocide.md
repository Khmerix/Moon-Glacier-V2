> **NARRATION**
> Kael didn't run. He unloaded every weapon he had into the breach—plasma bolts turning the wet walls to steam, the stench of cooked flesh and vaporized ammonia filling the air until he couldn't breathe without gagging.

> **NARRATION**
> The entity screamed. Not in pain. In betrayal. It had offered knowledge, and Kael had answered with fire.

> **NARRATION**
> He used the mining lasers next, carving trenches through the crystal heart, watching the silver filaments curl and blacken like hair in a flame. Then the thermite charges, melting the organic matrix into a soup of carbon and boiling protein. Then, when the entity finally screamed in real fear—a frequency that shattered his left eardrum and sent blood streaming down his neck—he used the nuclear payload.

> **NARRATION**
> The explosion was visible from orbit. A flower of white fire blooming across the glacier, turning ancient ice into steam, ancient flesh into ash. The shockwave threw Kael two hundred meters. He landed on his side, felt his collarbone snap, his hip dislocate, three fingers on his right hand crushed to paste beneath his own body.

> **NARRATION**
> He crawled back. Dragging himself through radioactive slag and melted ice, his legs useless, his vision narrowed to a red tunnel, he crawled back to make sure it was dead.

> **DIALOGUE: Kael Vance** *burnt*
> "It's done."

> **DIALOGUE: Elara Synn** *weeping*
> "We killed something older than life on Earth. Something that only wanted to talk. And we burned it because we were afraid."

> **NARRATION**
> Kael looked at his hands. They were blistered, blackened, shaking. Two fingers didn't respond anymore—nerve damage from the radiation. Human hands. Human, frightened, violent hands.

> **NARRATION**
> He had saved humanity from the unknown. He wondered, as the transport climbed toward the stars and the moon shrank behind them, whether he had also saved humanity from its own future.

> **NARRATION**
> Earth celebrated him as a hero. They gave him medals. They named schools after him.

> **NARRATION**
> But in the quiet moments, when Kael walked alone beneath Earth's sky with a cane and a missing ear and fingers that would never feel warmth again, he wondered what he had destroyed. And whether he had just made humanity the next monster in the dark.
