> **NARRATION**
> The ice gives way to a hollow larger than a stadium. Your suit lamps catch the walls and scatter into a thousand rainbows.

> **NARRATION**
> Crystals. Not terrestrial formations. These are geometric, angular, growing in spirals that suggest mathematical precision rather than geological chance. Some pulse with internal light—soft blues and violets that wax and wane like breathing.

> **DIALOGUE: Dr. Elara Chen** *awed*
> "These crystal lattices... they're not just mineral deposits. I'm reading organic compounds. Complex proteins. This structure is biological. It grew here."

> **NARRATION**
> Elara's scanner chirps wildly. She looks up, eyes wide behind her visor.

> **DIALOGUE: Dr. Elara Chen** *shocked*
> "The entire chamber is a single organism. Or was. These crystals are cellular structures. Kepler-442b didn't just evolve life in its oceans—it evolved architecture."

> **NARRATION**
> At the center of the chamber, the crystals form a dais. A platform. Waiting.

> **NARRATION**
> And on it: footprints. Human boots. Recent. The thermal signature is still warm.

> **DIALOGUE: Commander Voss** *grim*
> "Someone from the outpost found this place before us. And they came here deliberately."
