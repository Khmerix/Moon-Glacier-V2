> **NARRATION**
> Kael didn't hesitate. The entity had admitted to consuming civilizations, to trapping minds in eternal ice, to playing god with species it deemed "interesting."

> **NARRATION**
> It had to die.

> **NARRATION**
> He used the outpost's mining lasers first, carving trenches through the crystal heart. Then the thermite charges, melting the organic matrix. Then, when the entity finally screamed in real fear, he used the nuclear payload.

> **NARRATION**
> The moon cracked. Not destroyed—wounded. The entity retreated into the deepest ice, bleeding light, howling in frequencies that shattered satellites.

> **DIALOGUE: Kael Vance** *avenging*
> "You called us children. You were right. Children break things they don't understand. Remember that."

> **NARRATION**
> Earth celebrated him as a hero. The alien threat, eliminated. Humanity, safe. The stars, conquered.

> **NARRATION**
> But in the quiet moments, when Kael walked alone beneath Earth's sky, he wondered what he had destroyed. And whether he had just made humanity the next monster in the dark.
