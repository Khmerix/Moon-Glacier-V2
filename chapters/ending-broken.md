> **NARRATION**
> Kael attacked the entity with his bare hands. It was absurd. It was futile. It was the only thing he could do.

> **NARRATION**
> The entity didn't fight back. It simply absorbed him, wrapping crystalline tendrils around his fists, his shoulders, his screaming face.

> **DIALOGUE: The Entity** *sorrowful*
> "Your grief is beautiful, Commander. So pure. So human. We will keep it safe. We will keep you safe."

> **NARRATION**
> Kael felt his mind opening. Not painfully. Gently. Like a flower forced to bloom in winter.

> **NARRATION**
> He saw Sera. Not a memory. Sera. Alive. Smiling. Reaching for him.

> **DIALOGUE: Sera Voss** *welcoming*
> "You came. I knew you would."

> **NARRATION**
> Kael took her hand. The chamber was cold. The entity was warm. And Sera was waiting.

> **NARRATION**
> The team never found his body. They searched for weeks. The glacier gave up nothing.

> **NARRATION**
> But sometimes, on quiet nights, the outpost sensors detected two heartbeats in the deep ice. Beating in sync. Human and alien. Grief and love. The oldest rhythm in the universe.
