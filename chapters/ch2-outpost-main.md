> **NARRATION**
> The transport touches down hard, skidding across the permacrete pad before magnetic clamps seize the hull. The cabin fills with the scream of metal on ice.

> **NARRATION**
> Through the viewport: the outpost sprawls before you. A central hub of hexagonal modules connected by pressurized corridors, all of it dark except for the pulsing red emergency lights.

> **NARRATION**
> No movement. No personnel on the pad. The outpost should have a greeting party, a docking crew, someone.

> **DIALOGUE: Commander Voss** *quiet*
> "I'm reading life signs. Faint. Scattered. But the central comm array is dead. Someone cut the main antenna."

> **NARRATION**
> Voss powers down the transport. The silence that follows is deeper than space.

> **DIALOGUE: Commander Voss** *serious*
> "We need to reach Command. Find out what happened. Stay behind me, keep your sidearm ready."

> **NARRATION**
> The airlock cycles. The cold hits like a physical blow—minus forty, wind cutting through your suit's thermal layer. The outpost doors stand open. Not forced. Unlocked from inside.
