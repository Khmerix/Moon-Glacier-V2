> **NARRATION**
> Kael caught Marcus at the extraction chamber. Marcus had already begun the protocol—waking the entity with Helix frequencies, forcing it into consciousness before it was ready. The chamber vibrated with a sound below hearing, a pressure that made Kael's teeth ache and his vision pulse red at the edges.

> **NARRATION**
> The entity screamed. Not a sound. A force. The moon shook, and Kael understood with perfect clarity: if the protocol completed, the entity would lash out at everything within a hundred kilometers. Not at Marcus. At everything.

> **NARRATION**
> He didn't hesitate. He shot the control console, overloaded the power grid, and triggered the emergency collapse.

> **NARRATION**
> The ceiling came down in tons of ice and steel. The first beam caught Kael across the back, driving him to his knees. He felt ribs crack—sharp, distinct pops, like breaking branches. Air left his lungs and didn't come back. He tasted copper. Blood, running from his nose, his mouth, his ears.

> **DIALOGUE: Marcus Thorne** *buried*
> "Kael! You fool! You could have had everything!"

> **NARRATION**
> Kael tried to speak. Blood came out instead. The second beam pinned his legs. He felt the femur in his right thigh snap, the jagged end pressing against the inside of his skin like a finger probing a wound.

> **NARRATION**
> The pain was beyond screaming. It was a white wall that filled the universe. He focused on it. Used it. Found the overload switch through the haze and slammed his palm against it.

> **NARRATION**
> The explosion was silent to him. His eardrums had burst. But he felt the pressure wave, the heat, the sudden release as the entity's incomplete awakening collapsed in on itself.

> **DIALOGUE: Kael Vance** *smiling*
> "I did."

> **NARRATION**
> The team escaped. Kael did not. When they dug through the rubble three days later, they found his body wrapped around the shattered console, hand still on the overload switch.

> **NARRATION**
> His spine was broken in three places. Both legs were crushed to pulp. He had bled out slowly, pinned in the dark, listening to the ice settle around him.

> **NARRATION**
> The entity was saved. Humanity was saved. And Commander Kael Vance became the first human buried on a moon that was no longer just ice.

> **NARRATION**
> Sera placed his dog tags on the cairn. Elara wept. Nyx drank until she couldn't remember her own name.

> **NARRATION**
> Marcus Thorne's body was never found. Some said he was still down there, buried with Kael, screaming in the dark where no one could hear.
