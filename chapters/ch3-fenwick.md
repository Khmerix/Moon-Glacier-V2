> **NARRATION**
> They found Fenwick in the walls. Not behind them. In them.

> **NARRATION**
> Sub-Level 4 had been sealed for six months—biohazard quarantine, according to the rusted sign. Kael cut through the lock. The door opened with a wet sucking sound, like pulling a boot from mud.

> **NARRATION**
> The walls breathed. That was the first thing. The second was the smell—ammonia and overripe fruit, the smell of a body decomposing in summer heat, except the temperature read negative twelve.

> **NARRATION**
> Fenwick was suspended in the far wall, chest-deep in crystallized ice that had grown through his ribcage, his spine, his skull. His left eye was gone, replaced by a multifaceted crystal that caught the light and threw it back in colors that shouldn't exist.

> **DIALOGUE: Dr. Silas Fenwick** *gurgling*
> "Kael. Kael Vance. I remember you. Small. Human. Loud heartbeat. Thump-thump. Thump-thump. The moon likes that sound. It reminds it of something it used to feel."

> **NARRATION**
> His jaw moved wrong. The crystals had fused with the mandible, forcing it to open and close at angles that would have shattered a normal man's face.

> **DIALOGUE: Elara Synn** *whispering*
> "He's still conscious. The crystals are keeping him alive. Kael, he's been awake in there for six months."

> **DIALOGUE: Dr. Silas Fenwick** *laughing*
> "Six months? No, no. I've been here for twelve thousand years. I just didn't know it until the ice taught me. It teaches everyone, eventually. Even you, Marcus. Especially you."

> **NARRATION**
> Marcus went pale. Fenwick's crystal eye tracked him with mechanical precision.

> **DIALOGUE: Dr. Silas Fenwick** *prophetic*
> "The entity isn't a god. It's a farmer. And we're the crop. Marcus knows. Marcus has been fertilizing the soil."

> **NARRATION**
> Marcus's hand went to his sidearm. Kael saw it. Everyone saw it.
