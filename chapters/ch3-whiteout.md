> **NARRATION**
> The storm did not weaken. It grew teeth. For six hours, the team huddled in the damaged transport while the wind screamed against the hull like something trying to get in.

> **NARRATION**
> Nyx drifted in and out of consciousness. Sera never left her side, checking vitals, administering stimulants, whispering reassurances that neither of them believed.

> **NARRATION**
> Elara found the log. A data chip wedged in the transport's emergency beacon, hidden there by the previous expedition. What she played changed everything.

> **DIALOGUE: Elara Synn** *shaken*
> "It's the old team. They made contact. They... they tried to communicate with it. And it answered. But not with words. With dreams. With memories that weren't theirs."

> **NARRATION**
> The log ended in static. And screaming. But not human screaming. Something older. Something that had learned to scream by listening to stars die.

> **DIALOGUE: Kael Vance** *haunted*
> "How long do we wait? Nyx needs a med-bay. We have six hours of oxygen left if we stay here."

> **NARRATION**
> Outside, the wind stopped. Completely. The silence was worse than the storm.

> **DIALOGUE: Nyx Oriel** *weak*
> "Commander... it's not a storm. It's a breath. The moon is breathing. And it's awake now."
