> **NARRATION**
> Kael carried Sera's body himself. All ninety pounds of her, wrapped in a thermal blanket, through collapsing tunnels and screaming wind. He didn't let anyone else touch her.

> **NARRATION**
> The transport lifted off with a full manifest for the first time in days. No one spoke. No one looked at the moon shrinking below them.

> **DIALOGUE: Nyx Oriel** *quiet*
> "We failed, didn't we? The mission. The outpost. We failed."

> **DIALOGUE: Kael Vance** *empty*
> "The mission was to save lives. We saved four. We lost one. That's not failure. That's just... math."

> **NARRATION**
> Earth would ask questions. The board of inquiry would demand answers. Kael would tell them the truth: that GL-7 was dangerous, that the entity was real, that Sera Voss had died so the rest of them could live.

> **NARRATION**
> And then he would resign. Find a quiet place. Try to remember what sleep felt like before the moon.

> **NARRATION**
> Some missions change the world. Some missions just change you. And some missions take pieces of you that you never get back.

> **NARRATION**
> Kael held Sera's dog tags all the way home. He didn't let go until they landed.
