> **NARRATION**
> The transport banks hard, Voss fighting the controls as you skim the glacier surface at treetop height—if glaciers had trees.

> **NARRATION**
> The beacon sits atop a communications relay station, half-buried in drift ice. Its crimson light strobes across the white plain like a heartbeat.

> **DIALOGUE: Commander Voss** *strained*
> "I'm setting us down fifty meters out. The ice is unstable near the structure. We'll walk the rest."

> **NARRATION**
> The transport settles with a crunch of crystallized snow. You cycle the airlock and step onto an alien world.

> **NARRATION**
> The silence is absolute. No wind—Kepler-442b's atmosphere is too thin to carry sound. Your boots crunch on ice that predates human civilization.

> **NARRATION**
> The beacon tower looms. And at its base: a surface vehicle, tracks still fresh in the snow. Someone was here recently. The driver's door hangs open.

> **DIALOGUE: Commander Voss** *whispered*
> "Blood. On the door handle. Frozen solid."

> **NARRATION**
> Inside the cab: a tablet. Screen cracked but still glowing. Last entry timestamped fourteen hours ago.
